Various diseases can afflict fruit plants and their leaves, posing challenges to the health and productivity of these valuable crops. Powdery mildew, a fungal infection, manifests as a white powdery substance on the leaves, fruit, and stems of plants like apples, grapes, and strawberries, often leading to stunted growth. Apple scab, caused by the fungus Venturia inaequalis, results in olive-green to black spots on apple tree leaves, fruit, and twigs, potentially causing defoliation.
